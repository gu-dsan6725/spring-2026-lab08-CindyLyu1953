## PRIORITY 1: Internet (Comcast) — Potential Savings: $30–$45/month

**Phone:** 1-800-934-6489 (ask for Retention Department)
**Best Time:** Tuesday–Thursday, 10 AM–2 PM

### Script
> "Hi, I'm calling about my Comcast internet service. I've been a customer for several years, but my promotional rate has expired and my bill jumped to $89.99 per month. I've been looking at other options and wanted to see if we can work something out before I make a change."

> "I have three quotes right now: Charter Spectrum is showing $65 for 300 Mbps, and Verizon 5G Home is $50/month. Both would work for my needs. Before I go through the switching process, I wanted to give Comcast a chance."

**If they offer $79.99:**
> "I appreciate that, but that's still $30 more than Charter's offer. Can we get down to $59.99, or is there a bundle option with mobile that might help?"

**If they refuse:**
> "Can I speak with your supervisor or someone in retention who might have access to additional promotions? I'd really prefer to stay with Comcast if possible."

**Expected Outcome:** $25–$40/mo reduction ($300–$480/yr)

## PRIORITY 3: Mortgage Refinancing — Potential Savings: $50–$200+/month

**Refinance if:** Current rate is above 6.5% AND you plan to stay 5+ years

### Script for Current Lender
> "Hi, I'm a current mortgage holder. I've received refinancing offers from three lenders at rates around [X]% with closing costs around $[Y]. Before I move my mortgage, I wanted to see what Acme can offer me — and whether you can waive the appraisal or origination fee given my payment history."

**Break-even calculation:** Closing costs ÷ Monthly savings = Months to break even
- Example: $5,000 ÷ $150/mo = 33 months (worthwhile if staying 3+ years)

**Expected Outcome:** $75–$150/mo savings ($900–$1,800/yr) — depends heavily on current rate

## Negotiation Golden Rules

### DO:
- Have real competitor quotes **before** calling
- Say: "I have a specific offer from [Competitor] for [exact amount]"
- Say: "I've been a loyal customer and want to stay, but the rate needs to work"
- Ask to speak with a supervisor if initial rep can't help
- Actually follow through on switching if negotiation fails

### DON'T:
- Say "I can't afford this" (they'll offer downgrades, not discounts)
- Say "Can you do anything?" (too vague)
- Call on Monday/Friday or early morning
- Reveal you're calling multiple providers at once
